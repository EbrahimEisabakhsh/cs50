Capacitor

A capacitor is used to temporarily store an electrical charge and inevitably release
that built up charge, quite rapidly (often within seconds or less). Why is this
beneficial you may ask? Capacitors are an essential part to the operation of the
electrical devices and machines which we rely on.  Whether it be Dr. Emmett “Doc”
Brown’s fictional DeLorean flux-capacitor or even a storm cloud above our very own
heads, chances it is quite possible that you’ve seen the concept of a capacitor in
one form or another.
